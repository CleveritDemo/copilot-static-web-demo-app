The Sun

The Sun is a star at the center of our solar system. It is an almost perfect sphere of hot plasma, and its gravity keeps the planets in orbit around it.

Planets of the Solar System

  • Mercury: The closest planet to the Sun, known for its extreme temperature.
  • Venus: Often called Earth's "twin", it has a dense and hot atmosphere.
  • Earth: Our home, the only planet known to harbor life.
  • Mars: Known as the "red planet", it has features that suggest it may have harbored life.
  • Jupiter: The largest planet in the solar system, famous for its Great Red Spot.
  • Saturn: Known for its impressive rings, it is the second largest planet.
  • Uranus: A gas giant that rotates on its side, with a distinctive blue color due to methane in its atmosphere.
  • Neptune: The farthest planet from the Sun, known for its strong winds and deep blue color.
  • Pluto: Although no longer classified as a major planet, it remains a fascinating object in the solar system.
